The First Cooking

For the next step, we will be creating a soffritto. This is a classic base in Italian and Spanish cooking. First, take your Vidalia onion and fine dice it. One whole onion should give you about one cup of onions. Make sure you do not use any of the root area. Next, mince six cloves of garlic, or enough to give you five tablespoons. In the stock pot, heat a third cup of extra virgin olive oil over medium-low heat. Add your diced onion. Stir the onions almost the entire time. Make sure your heat is not so hot that the onions are browning. Once the onions begin to become translucent, add the minced garlic. We want to make sure that neither the onions or garlic burn. At this point, a light brown color is good, but if it turns black, you will have to start over as this will ruin the sauce. That is a basic soffritto.

Once a light brown color has been achieved, stir in one cup of the red wine. Let this mixture cook until the wine has been reduced in volume by about two thirds. When the wine has reduced enough, add all of the tomatoes and juices. Cook this over low heat for about two hours. Make sure you are stirring every 10 to 15 minutes. When stirring, get everything up from off the bottom of the pan. If it is starting to burn, turn your heat down. If at any point, the sauce seems too thick, you may add a little water to thin it back out. Once the tomatoes have cooked down, process the sauce in small batches in your food mill. If you are using a food processor for this step, make sure that you do not over fill the bowl as it might cause an explosion of sauce.
