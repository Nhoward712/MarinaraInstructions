Now that all of the sauce has been passed through the food mill, return it to the stove for the final step. On a clean cutting board take fifteen to twenty basil leaves and cut them chiffonade (Figure 2). At this point, taste the sauce and add salt to your liking. Stir the sauce well and serve over your favorite pasta immediately. If you are not planning on using the sauce right away, do not cut or add the basil. This can be done at the t ime of service once the sauce is reheated.

Now that you have an understanding of how a traditional Italian marinara sauce is made, you can use your own imagination to modify it to your liking. Some people like their sauce heavy on the garlic. I like to add a handful of grated parmesan cheese right at the end. Maybe using sun-dried tomatoes would be a good thing. Who knows? The possibilities are nearly endless. Whatever experiments you try, just remember that there is no wrong way as long as you like the results.
