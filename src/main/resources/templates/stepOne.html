Gather Your Ingredients

Next, we need to gather all of our ingredients. While all of these ingredients are readily available at your local supermarket, it is always best to pick the produce from your own garden in the late fall when the flavor is best. The main ingredient in marinara is tomatoes. Fresh is best, although you can sometimes find good canned San Marzano tomatoes in specialty food stores. Roma tomatoes work well also because of their flavor and their moisture content. If Roma tomatoes are used, they should be fresh, not canned. Other types of tomatoes will work, but will not produce superior results. One of the things that can make or break your sauce is the quality of basil that you are using. First, don’t ever use dried basil. Freshly picked is the best option. If you need to use pre picked, make sure the leaves you are using don’t have any dark spots on them. This will make your sauce bitter.

For one of the early steps, you will be creating a soffrito, which is a mixture of onions and garlic. Make sure that you are using sweet Vidalia onions as the higher sugar content will help when it comes time to caramelize the onions. Most types of fresh garlic will work for this sauce as long as it is fresh and has the ends where it connects to the base removed. Do not use jarred or powdered garlic under any circumstances. The flavor will become dulled and bitter if you do. You will need a good quality extra virgin olive oil. If price is an issue and you need to purchase a lesser quality, that will be ok. With the amount that is used, it won’t affect the sauce significantly.

The last ingredient we need to source is red wine. Most red wines will work as long is they are not a fortified wine, like port wine. Any quality of cabernet sauvignon will work well. Quality of the wine is not as important here because most of it will be cooked out anyway. Just make sure that you find a wine that you will enjoy drinking because there will be leftovers after you open the bottle.
